QuickQuest Tutorial

QuickQuest is all about speed, creativity, and teamwork.

When you create a character, you will be given random weapons, spells, and passives, and it's your job to use what you're given and complete a quest with your team.

Unless specified, use a D20 for a roll. Rolls usually require a modifier as well (like 1D4 + END - meaning roll 1 D4 and add your END value)
Some rolls may require a proficiency (like a Perception roll - meaning roll 1 D20 and add your Perception bonus. If you don't have any Perception, it's just a regular D20 roll)

Your spells and actions in the game have tags (like [Attack] [Spell] [Fire], etc). These tags tell you information about the action.

  • [Attack] rolls are attacks and you use 1D20 + AGI to see if it hits
  • [Spell] rolls are spells and you use 1D20 + WIT to see if it hits
  • [Fire] actions can burn stuff and make fire
  • [Water] actions can create water and extinguish stuff
  • [Terrain] actions can create terrain
  • [Poison] actions can poison stuff
  • [Light] actions can make light
If your action doesn't have an [Attack] or [Spell] tag, then it automatically hits!

When being hit by [Attack]s, the attacker needs to make an [Attack] roll and get higher than your Swiftness.
When being hit by [Spell]s, the caster needs to make a [Spell] roll and get higher than your Spirit.

When attacking an enemy, you need to make an [Attack] roll that is equal to or more than their Swiftness.
When casting an enemy, you need to make a [Spell] roll that is equal to or more than their Spirit.
